# Product

## Register

product

## Users

DataAutomated serves SaaS and ecommerce operators who need interpreted customer, competitor, and journey intelligence without hiring an internal data team. Primary users are founders, growth leaders, customer success leads, product leaders, and analysts at SaaS companies from roughly $500K to $15M ARR and ecommerce brands from roughly $2M to $25M GMV.

Users are usually in an operating workflow: connecting data sources, checking whether agents have produced new intelligence, reviewing churn or competitor risks, downloading reports, and sharing findings with teammates.

## Product Purpose

DataAutomated turns customer feedback, competitive signals, and behavioral journey data into interpreted business intelligence. The product exists to close three gaps: scattered customer voice, late competitor awareness, and dashboards that show drop-off without explaining why.

Success means a customer can sign up, create an organization, connect real data sources, add competitors, receive real insights from their own data, download reports, receive scheduled deliveries, and operate the product without developer intervention.

## Brand Personality

The product should feel expert, calm, and operationally credible. It should communicate that the system is rigorous enough for sensitive business intelligence, direct enough for busy operators, and honest about what is connected, processing, empty, failed, or ready.

## Anti-references

The product should not look like a decorative AI dashboard with fake metrics, vague gradients, dead buttons, or optimistic placeholder data. It should avoid consumer-style onboarding theatrics, over-designed cards, misleading success states, and any UI that implies integrations or reports are working before real validation has passed.

## Design Principles

1. Show operating truth: every screen should distinguish connected, disconnected, processing, failed, empty, and ready states.
2. Make the next action obvious: empty states and errors should tell the user exactly what to connect, retry, verify, or configure.
3. Preserve trust over polish: real data and honest gaps are more valuable than impressive-looking demo values.
4. Keep workflows dense but calm: operators should be able to scan KPIs, sources, competitors, jobs, and reports without visual noise.
5. Design for self-service: onboarding, source setup, teammate invites, and first report generation should not require developer intervention.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA for product UI. Maintain readable contrast, keyboard-accessible controls, visible focus states, reduced-motion support for nonessential animation, and clear text labels for status, error, and recovery states.
